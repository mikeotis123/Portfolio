My Recommendations For On line Schools

TreeHouse

Treehouse is probably my favorite. The reason I would recommend this site is that for $25 a month, you have an unlimited amount of coding material with projects that you can use for your own portfolio. Its like going to a big superstore for material. They have all the popular languages and just a vast amount of learning material. I have been going to them for little bit more then a year now and they cover every thing in the industry. From front end development to back end, to free lance to getting your first job in web development. There are a lot of experts on this site and I would highly recommend someone looking for material to sign up on there site.

CodeCademy

CodeCademy is also a great on line source for material. The don't cover as much material but they do have a lot of material that covers a lot of the popular coding languages out there. with CodeCademy you can go over the mass amount of material they offer for free, or you can pay a small monthly fee. I like this site because when I get stuck on treehouse I come over to this site and see if they can show me a different point of view. This site is a very good site to learn front-end and back-end technology's.

FreeCodeCamp

This is one of the first sites that I found that covers a lot of material in one place. This site keeps adding learning material. I love this site for the same reasons I like the other sites I mentioned above. The material is all there to get you started. There are a bunch of coding examples and you can take some of the material and put it into your portfolio. This site offers a lot of great advise. Not to mention you do not have to pay for the material to learn a coding language. The biggest take away from teaching your self how to code is practice practice practice.

How To Succeed

The first thing you have to make sure you do when teaching your self how to code is practice practice practice. You can have all the material in the world, but if you don't practice what you learn you will not retain the information as well.

The second thing you should do when teaching your self how to code is, make sure you have enough sources to go to to learn. For example. I have four different places I go to to get information to learn. That way if I get stuck I can go to the other source and see if they can show me a different way of learning the material. I use tree house, codecademy, codecamp, and on occasion look through some of the courses on udemy. But do not get discouraged if you get stuck. Stay the course and keep plugging away. Eventually you will get it. Be as active in your learning as possible.

The biggest thing that you will hear from any one who has taught them selfs how to code is to get involved in a project. The key to staying motivated and to keep learning new skills is to work on a project that inspires you. When you help out with a project or do something on your own, you are putting your skills to use and keeping your self motivated to learn.

I love to code and continue to learn everyday. You have to evolve with the technology as it progresses. Also be active on the forums. Ask questions. If you don't know a word. Google it. Google will be your best friend when comes to being a self taught coder.
